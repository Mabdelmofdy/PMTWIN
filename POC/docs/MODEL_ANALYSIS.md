## Overlap Analysis

### Potential Overlaps Identified and Resolved:

#### 1. **1.1 Task-Based Engagement vs 4.2 Consultant Hiring**
**Similarity:** Both involve short-term expert services  
**Distinction:**
- **1.1** is broader: tasks, deliverables, consultation, design work, analysis (B2B, B2P, P2B, P2P)
- **4.2** is specific: consultant advisory services, expert opinions, specialized analysis (B2B, B2P, P2B, P2P)
- **1.1** focuses on task completion with deliverables
- **4.2** focuses on advisory/consultation services
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - 1.1 is task-oriented, 4.2 is advisory-oriented

#### 2. **1.2 Consortium vs 1.3 Project-Specific JV**
**Similarity:** Both involve partnerships for projects  
**Distinction:**
- **1.2** is a temporary contractual alliance (no new entity) for bidding/executing
- **1.3** is a formal JV partnership (may create new entity) with equity split
- **1.2** is typically for tenders/bids with multiple members
- **1.3** is typically for a specific project with defined equity
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different legal structures and purposes

#### 3. **1.3 Project-Specific JV vs 2.1 Strategic JV**
**Similarity:** Both involve Joint Ventures  
**Distinction:**
- **1.3** is project-specific with clear end (dissolution after project)
- **2.1** is strategic and long-term (ongoing business, no defined end)
- **1.3** focuses on single project execution
- **2.1** focuses on ongoing strategic business
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different timeframes and purposes

#### 4. **2.1 Strategic JV vs 2.2 Long-Term Strategic Alliance**
**Similarity:** Both are long-term strategic partnerships  
**Distinction:**
- **2.1** creates a new legal entity (incorporated JV)
- **2.2** is a partnership without forming new entity (contractual alliance)
- **2.1** involves equity, capital contribution, governance structure
- **2.2** involves preferred supplier, technology licensing, market access agreements
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different legal structures

#### 5. **3.1 Bulk Purchasing vs 3.3 Resource Sharing & Exchange**
**Similarity:** Both involve resource transactions  
**Distinction:**
- **3.1** is specifically group buying for volume discounts (buying new)
- **3.3** is a marketplace for selling/buying/renting/bartering resources (new or used)
- **3.1** focuses on collective purchasing power
- **3.3** focuses on resource exchange marketplace
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different transaction types and purposes

#### 6. **3.2 Co-Ownership Pooling vs 3.3 Resource Sharing & Exchange**
**Similarity:** Both involve sharing resources  
**Distinction:**
- **3.2** is joint purchase and co-ownership of expensive assets (equity-based)
- **3.3** is marketplace for transactions (sell/buy/rent/barter)
- **3.2** involves shared ownership and ongoing cost sharing
- **3.3** involves one-time or rental transactions
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different ownership models

#### 7. **4.1 Professional Hiring vs 4.2 Consultant Hiring**
**Similarity:** Both involve hiring professionals  
**Distinction:**
- **4.1** is employment (full-time, part-time, contract) with ongoing work
- **4.2** is consultant engagement for advisory services with specific deliverables
- **4.1** focuses on employment relationship
- **4.2** focuses on service engagement
- **Verdict:** ✅ Distinct - Different employment vs service models
